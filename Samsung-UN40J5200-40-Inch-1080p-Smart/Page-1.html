5.0 out of 5 starsExcellent quality, easy to set up and use
ByTOPJOB7VINE VOICEon August 21, 2015
Size: 32-InchStyle: TVVine Customer Review of Free Product( What's this? )
This TV is very light and extremely easy to set up. The foot design is surprisingly stable, so there's very little danger that this thing will get knocked over. Overall, it seems like a very high-quality product. I only had one issue: when I slid the TV across my dresser, one of the rubber pads came off and disappeared. I contacted Samsung, and they sent me a new set of feet at no cost.

When placed on a flat surface (like a dresser), the feet lift the TV by about 1.5", which is plenty of room for most blu-ray players to fit underneath. You can also mount it on your wall, but you'll need to buy a wall mounting kit (not included).

The sound quality is pretty good for a TV, and at 50% volume it's loud enough for my needs.

The TV comes with Amazon Instant Video pre-installed, and the TV also has built-in WiFi which is fast enough to stream HD video without any lag. It also comes with YouTube, Hulu, and Netflix, so you can watch stuff from those sites as well. The TV can also download new apps from the internet, if there's something you need that isn't already preinstalled on the TV. This is all easily accessible using the remote.

The TV only takes a couple of seconds to turn on, so I can start watching movies from my blu-ray player right away. However, there's one slight annoyance: it takes a full minute before SmartHub is ready and the contained apps are ready to use. So if you want to watch something from Amazon Instant Video, you have to wait for another minute after powering on.

On the back, the TV has the following ports:
* Component audio/video in (several ports)
* two HDMI in ports
* ANT (TV) in
* USB 3.0
* LAN (Local Area Network)
* Digital optical audio out

I've only needed to use the HDMI port, but the audio out will be useful if I ever want to upgrade to a high quality speaker system.

5.0 out of 5 starsSamsung 32-inch smart LED TV is a fine TV
ByBW & ABAPVINE VOICEon July 26, 2015
Size: 32-InchStyle: TVVine Customer Review of Free Product( What's this? )
I got this TV for the primary purpose of displaying vertical slideshows; like they do in malls and department stores. The TV does a great job for this purpose. More on this later.

TV comes with feet not assembled. Assembling the feet is is quick and easy. Yes, feet are made of plastic, very light, and feel weak/flimsy. However, they work great because the TV is very light as well.

The TV comes with VESA 200x200 mounting holes. This is very important for me to do the project that I had in mind. Regardless of VESA standard says, this TV requires the mounting screws to be M8x32mm long IF you are using Samsung mount. I am not using Samsung mount, so I had to use additional washers and spring washers.

While the TV is very light, it is not thin, though. I was hoping it would be much thinner than it is, because our 70" Vizio TV is very thin. This is somewhat disappointing to me, but doesn't hurt my project.

Connecting to wireless network is as easy as any other digital device. It will scan and present you available wireless networks, and you choose one and provide necessary credentials. Once connected to the network, I was able to browse the Internet and utilize some of the smart features. By the way, the browser use requires patience as pointer is moved by the arrow keys on the remote and typing is done by navigating the on-screen keyboard using remote's arrow keys. The browser works fine for general Internet browsing, but I don't know what is its HTML compliance.

Picture quality is great and sound quality is fine for a 32" TV.

Now, my project:
As I mentioned, I wanted to use this TV in a vertical position and display photo slideshow. Before fabricating the vertical stand, I first wanted to see how it looks and how it plays photos vertically.
- Picture quality and viewing angle are quite good when the display is turned to vertical position
- Photos can be on your flash drive and the TV can display them in a slideshow. You can even choose random transitions and it does have many standard transitions. Nothing fancy, though. For example, no zooming and panning during slideshow.
- TV does NOT have a feature to turn the picture vertically (like a Windows computer does.) Your pictures must be vertical. So, I had to take my pictures to Photoshop and turn them 90 degrees and save. Not a big hassle, but I would have been ecstatic if TV had a feature to turn the picture.

After I was satisfied with what the TV can do, I went and bought the hardware and assembled to the TV. See the pictures I have posted. I am happy with the final effect. Family was happy too. I am anxious to see how friends will react during the next gathering at our home.

2.0 out of 5 starsExtremely s..l...o...w TV
ByJ. Petersonon November 21, 2015
Size: 50-InchStyle: TVVerified Purchase
I've had (and still have) several other Samsung TVs, and have liked them. This one has the same good picture quality as my previous units, but I must say that it is the least satisfying TV that I have ever owned. Why? Because it is ...... slow.

How can a TV be slow?

It's slow to turn on. Remember picture tube TVs that used to take time to warm up? Not quite that bad, but takes time.
It's slow to change channels. You press a button on the remote and then wonder if the TV heard you, or not. Eventually it responds, but you are not sure if and when it will do that.
The "Smart TV" feature is particularly slow. Many times when trying to start viewing Netflix, this TV will put a pop-up on the screen that says something like "Preparing TV". This goes on and on. The only real relief I have found is to turn the TV off and then on again.
And then once the TV has been "prepared" for watching Netflix, it will go into an endless cycle of showing a few seconds of a show, then freeze while a spinning counter slowly goes up to 100%, shows a few more seconds, then repeats. Again, the only solution seems to be turning the unit off then on again.

This TV is just like having a big, bright monitor that is hooked to an old, really slow computer - that sometimes needs to be re-booted over and over again to correct problems.

Very, very disappointing!
